Pulao

Description

Popular rice dishes in the Middle East, Central Asia, and South Asia include pulao, which is also spelled pilaf or pilau. It is produced by simmering rice in stock or broth while frequently include meat, veggies, and seasonings. Although the history of pulao is poorly known, it is thought to have started in the Middle East and traveled throughout the world via trade and cultural interchange.

To begin making pulao, you must first cook the rice in a saucepan with water or broth. As the rice cooks, you can add veggies like peas, carrots, and onions as well as spices like cumin, cardamom, and cloves. You can sauté the meat separately before adding it to the saucepan with the rice and vegetables if you're using it. The pulao can be served hot and topped with fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro. Along with a protein like chicken or lamb, it frequently comes with a side of yogurt or raita. A bigger range of foods may include pulao in addition to being served as a main course.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups Basmati rice
  • 2 Tablespoon Ghee
  • 1 Teaspoon Oil
  • 1 Medium Onion
  • 2 Green Chilies
  • ½ Tablespoon Garlic
  • ½ Tablespoon Ginger
  • 1 small Tomato
  • ½ Cup Potato
  • ½ Cup Beans
  • ½ Cup Carrot
  • ½ Cup Green Peas
  • ⅓ cup Coriander leaves
  • ¼ cup Mint Leaves
  • 1 teaspoon Garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cup Water

Steps

  1. Set Instant Pot to Saute mode. Once hot add oil and ghee.
  2. Add all of the entire spices and cook for 30 seconds to release their aroma.
  3. Cook the chopped onion after adding it until it softens and begins to brown.
  4. Add chopped Potatoes. Cook for 2 minutes stirring in between.
  5. Crush some garlic and ginger. For 30 seconds, cook.
  6. Chopped carrot, beans, peas, tomatoes, and green chilies should then be added (if using). Mix everything thoroughly.
  7. Add the drained rice along with the chopped coriander and mint. After thoroughly combining everything, roast rice for two minutes. Gently mix between each interval to prevent burning, being careful not to break the grains.
  8. Include warm water. Deglaze the pot's bottom to remove any sticky material that has become lodged there. When seasoning is finished, add salt and taste.
  9. Put the IP in Manual/High-Pressure mode and close the lid. Set the clock for seven minutes.
  10. Allow the pressure to naturally release for ten minutes after the timer has finished. Then manually release the last presser, or rapid release.
  11. Serve with some cashews that have been toasted in ghee.
